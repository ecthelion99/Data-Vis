Australian Energy Generation

Energy generation throughout the Australian states have somewhat diverse sources. Over-time generation has become more diverse. Most states still have a significant reliance on either coal or natural gas. The two exceptions are Tasmania, which has almost no reliance on coal or gas, and South Australia, who's major generation source is wind and solar (a significant shift from 2019).

Australia's 2030 renewable goal is 82% renewable generation. Only Tasmania has achieved this (although Tasmania imports from the National Electricity Market) and South Australian is making good progress whilst the other states are lagging.

This map illustrates the total energy generation per capita across regions. Major energy-producing regions stand out, revealing the areas that may contribute significantly to the national or regional energy supply. By comparing this map with the fossil fuel consumption map, one can observe patterns, such as whether regions with high energy production also have high fossil fuel reliance, or if they are investing in cleaner energy alternatives.

This map allows us to compare the energy generation of Australia with the rest of the world. Being a developed country our our per-capita energy generation is relatively high, near that of the United States.
